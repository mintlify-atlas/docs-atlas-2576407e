---
title: "Conduct and score candidate interviews"
description: "View your interview assignments, open the interview scoring page, enter scores and notes against the interview rubric, and submit your feedback."
---

Interviews are the second stage of the application pipeline. Only applicants whose internal status has been set to `ReviewStatus.Interview` by an admin appear in the **Assigned Interviews** tab. When an interview is assigned to you, an `InterviewAssignment` record is created linking your account to the applicant's submitted response. You open the interview page, score the candidate against the interview rubric, and submit your feedback.

## Who appears in the interviews tab

The interviews dashboard only shows applicants who have reached the interview stage. This is controlled by the `ReviewStatus` on the applicant's `InternalApplicationStatus` record:

```typescript types.ts
export enum ReviewStatus {
  NotReviewed = "not-reviewed",
  UnderReview = "under-review",
  Reviewed = "reviewed",
  Interview = "interview",   // only applicants at this status appear in interviews
  Accepted = "accepted",
  Denied = "denied",
  Waitlisted = "waitlist",
}
```

<Info>
  Advancing an applicant to interview status is an admin action. If you expect to see an applicant in your interviews list but they are missing, ask a board member or super reviewer to check the applicant's status.
</Info>

## Interview assignments

Each interview you are assigned is represented by an `InterviewAssignment` object:

```typescript types.ts
export type InterviewAssignment = {
  id: string;
  assignmentType: "interview";
  formId: string;
  interviewerId: string;
  applicantId: string;
  applicationResponseId: string;
  forRole: ApplicantRole;
};
```

All your assignments for the active form appear in the **Assigned Interviews** tab of the reviewer dashboard. The three summary cards show:

- **Interviews** — total assigned
- **Completed** — assignments where a submitted `ApplicationInterviewData` exists
- **Pending** — assignments not yet submitted

## Opening the interview page

Click a row in the Assigned Interviews table to open the interview scoring page at:

```
/admin/interview/f/:formId/:responseId/:interviewDataId
```

The three URL segments are:

| Segment | Description |
| --- | --- |
| `formId` | The active application form |
| `responseId` | The applicant's submitted `ApplicationResponse` |
| `interviewDataId` | Your `ApplicationInterviewData` record for this assignment |

## The interview page layout

The interview page is structurally identical to the application review page: a resizable split view with the applicant's form responses on the left and the interview rubric on the right.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Left panel — application" icon="file">
    Read-only view of the applicant's submitted responses. Sections with `hideFromReviewers: true` are hidden, and role-specific sections filter by the applicant's applied roles.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Right panel — interview rubric" icon="list-checks">
    One or more interview rubric cards. Enter a numeric score for each question and optional notes. Scores are saved when you submit; notes autosave after one second of inactivity.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Press `Cmd+K` (or `Ctrl+K`) to open the section jump palette and scroll to any section of the form by name.

## The `ApplicationInterviewData` structure

Your interview scores and notes are stored in a single `ApplicationInterviewData` document:

```typescript types.ts
export interface ApplicationInterviewData {
  id: string;
  interviewerId: string;
  applicationFormId: string;
  applicationResponseId: string;
  applicantId: string;
  interviewScores: Record<string, number>;   // keyed by rubric scoreKey
  interviewerNotes: Record<string, string>;  // keyed by rubric scoreKey
  forRole: ApplicantRole;
  submitted: boolean;
}
```

`interviewScores` and `interviewerNotes` are both plain objects keyed by the `scoreKey` of each `ReviewRubricQuestion` in the interview rubric. The same rubric type (`RoleReviewRubric`) is used for both application reviews and interviews.

## Filling in interview scores and notes

For each question in the interview rubric:

1. Enter a numeric score within the `minValue`–`maxValue` range defined on the question (default 0–4).
2. Optionally type notes in the comment field below each score.

Notes autosave one second after you stop typing. The header displays **Last saved [time]** or **Not saved** depending on autosave state.

<Warning>
  Only notes autosave. Score selections are not persisted until you submit. Do not close or navigate away from the page before submitting.
</Warning>

The running **Interview Score** shown in the header is a weighted aggregate based on `interviewScoreWeights` configured on the form — it updates as you enter scores and gives you a preview before submission.

## Submitting interview feedback

<Steps>
  <Step title="Score every rubric question">
    Each `scoreKey` from every interview rubric question must have an entry in `interviewScores`. If any key is missing, the portal shows an error toast and blocks submission.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Submit Interview">
    Click the **Submit Interview** button in the blue header bar at the top of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm in the dialog">
    The confirmation dialog reads "You will not be able to edit it after submission." Click **Confirm** to finalize.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Feedback saved">
    A success toast confirms the submission and the portal navigates back to your dashboard. The assignment moves from **Pending** to **Completed** in the summary cards.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Submitted interviews are locked. Once `submitted` is `true`, all score inputs and note fields become disabled. Contact a super reviewer if you need to amend a submitted interview.
</Warning>

## Internal applicants

Applicants with `isInternal: true` on their profile are returning club members who skip the standard application review and enter the pipeline directly at the interview stage. The interview page header displays an **INTERNAL** badge next to their name so you can identify them quickly.
